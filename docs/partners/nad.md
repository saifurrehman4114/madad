# Outreach letter — National Association of the Deaf, Pakistan

**To:** President, National Association of the Deaf (NAD) Pakistan
**Subject:** PSL accuracy review for a new open-source interpreter

---

Respected President,

Assalam-o-alaikum. My name is Saif Ur Rehman, a senior software engineer
based in Lahore.

I have built **Madad**, an offline Pakistan Sign Language interpreter that
runs entirely on a mid-range Android phone using Google DeepMind's Gemma 4
open model. Madad does PSL → spoken Urdu / English, and Urdu / English →
a signing 3D avatar, with no internet connection required.

I am submitting Madad to the Gemma 4 Good Hackathon, but more importantly,
I am writing to ask two things of NAD before a public v1.0 release:

1. **A PSL accuracy review** by NAD-appointed signers of the 100-sign
   benchmark I have recorded (PSL-100). I am happy to revise any sign or
   gloss the NAD review team flags as non-standard.
2. **NAD endorsement or participation** in defining what a v1.0 release
   criteria must look like — what sign coverage, what accuracy floor,
   what documentation in PSL video.

The full source is Apache-2.0, the benchmark is CC-BY 4.0, and I am
contributing the adapter weights to the public domain regardless of
competition outcome. No data ever leaves a user's phone — there are no
analytics and no account required.

I would be grateful for a brief meeting (online or in person in
Islamabad) at NAD's convenience.

Respectfully,
