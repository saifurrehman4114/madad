**Subject:** Offering a free offline PSL interpreter APK for Deaf Reach students

Dear Mr. Geary,

My name is Saif Ur Rehman, a software engineer in Lahore. I have built
**Madad** — an offline Pakistan Sign Language interpreter that runs on any
mid-range Android phone, in airplane mode, powered by Google's recently
released Gemma 4 open model.

Madad does two things end-to-end on-device:

1. A Deaf user signs for four seconds into the phone camera; Madad
   produces a live Urdu caption and reads it aloud for the hearing
   listener.
2. A hearing person speaks Urdu or English; a 3D avatar on the screen
   signs the reply in PSL.

I am submitting Madad to Google DeepMind's **Gemma 4 Good Hackathon**, and
more importantly, I am asking if Deaf Reach would be willing to host a
**25-student pilot at the Rashidabad campus** between May and July 2026.
The app and source code are licensed Apache 2.0 and CC-BY 4.0 — no
strings, no telemetry, no data leaves students' phones.

**What I would ask of FESF:**
- Permission to install the APK on 25 student phones (we supply the
  engineer for the install session).
- A 30-minute orientation with one of your PSL interpreters present.
- Candid feedback over four weeks on what Madad gets right and wrong for
  the Deaf-Reach dialect of PSL.

**What FESF would get:**
- Full source, full ownership of any follow-on model fine-tuned on
  Deaf-Reach data (with appropriate student consent).
- Joint publication credit on any academic write-up.
- The right to veto any use of FESF's name in promotional material.

I am happy to drive to Rashidabad for an introductory meeting. If helpful,
a public demo video is at madad.vercel.app.
